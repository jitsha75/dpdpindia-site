DPDP Act Compliance for Indian Businesses

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 imposes legal obligations on any entity that collects, processes, or stores personal data of Indian citizens. Non-compliance risks penalties up to ₹500 Crore. Here is your complete toolkit — sized for your organisation.

By Organisation Type

Pick Your Compliance Path

The DPDP Act applies to all businesses processing personal data — but your implementation priority and complexity scales with your size and data volume.

Startup · <50 employees
Startup Compliance
If you run an app, website, or SaaS product that collects any personal data from Indian users — you are a Data Fiduciary under DPDP Act. Here's what you must do first.
Your Exposure
Up to ₹50 Cr for failure to notify breach. Up to ₹200 Cr for ignoring user rights requests. These apply regardless of company size.
  • Audit every data collection point in your product
  • Add a compliant privacy policy (use our template)
  • Implement explicit consent before data collection
  • Build a DSR (Data Subject Request) handling process
  • Appoint a Data Protection Officer (if applicable)
  • Set up a breach detection + 72-hour notification process
Startup Checklist →
SME · 50–500 employees
SME Compliance Kit
For SMEs with customer databases, HR data, and vendor relationships — DPDP compliance requires a structured approach across data categories and departments.
Your Exposure
Customer data breaches can trigger ₹250 Cr penalty. HR data mishandling opens both DPDP and labour law liability.
  • Data inventory — map all personal data flows
  • Classify data by category (customer, HR, vendor)
  • Update all consent notices and privacy policies
  • Vendor/processor contracts with DPDP clauses
  • Staff training on data handling basics
  • Incident response plan for data breaches
  • Internal grievance mechanism for DSRs
SME Compliance Kit →
Enterprise · 500+ employees
Enterprise Framework
Large enterprises may qualify as Significant Data Fiduciaries (SDF), attracting additional obligations including mandatory DPO, DPIA, and data localisation considerations.
Your Exposure
As a potential SDF: mandatory annual data audit, DPIA for high-risk processing, DPO requirement, and enhanced breach obligations. Non-compliance = up to ₹500 Cr.
  • SDF assessment — volume and sensitivity criteria
  • Appoint a Data Protection Officer (mandatory for SDF)
  • Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)
  • Cross-border data transfer framework
  • Annual data audit by independent auditor
  • Board-level data governance policy
  • Data localisation readiness for restricted categories
  • Legal basis mapping for all processing activities

Phase 1 — Foundation (Week 1–2)
Understand Your Obligations
  • Determine if you are a Data Fiduciary (you collect/process personal data of Indian citizens)Critical
  • Identify all data collection points (web forms, app, cookies, APIs, third-party SDKs)Critical
  • Classify personal data by category (general, children's data, sensitive categories)Critical
  • Assess if you may qualify as Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF) and what additional obligations applyImportant
  • Map your data processors — any vendors or partners you share data withImportant
Phase 2 — Documentation (Week 3–4)
Get Your Documents Right
  • Draft or update Privacy Policy to be DPDP-compliant (use our template below)Critical
  • Create DPDP-compliant Consent Notice — itemised purpose, no bundled consentCritical
  • Add consent mechanism before data collection begins (not buried in T&Cs)Critical
  • Update vendor/partner contracts to include Data Processing Agreements (DPA)Important
  • Document your lawful basis for each data processing activityImportant
Phase 3 — Operationalise (Month 2)
Build Operational Processes
  • Build a Data Subject Request (DSR) handling process — 30-day response windowCritical
  • Implement consent withdrawal mechanism — as easy as giving consentCritical
  • Create internal grievance mechanism with named Grievance OfficerImportant
  • Implement data breach detection and 72-hour notification processCritical
  • Staff training on DPDP Act basics for all employees handling personal dataImportant
Phase 4 — Ongoing (Month 3+)
Maintain Compliance
  • Quarterly review of data collection practices and vendor listOngoing
  • Annual data audit (mandatory for SDFs, best practice for all)Important
  • Monitor DPDPB notifications and update practices accordinglyOngoing
  • Register on DPDPB portal when operational and submit required recordsImportant
  • Subscribe to DPDPIndia.in Monthly Brief for regulatory updatesRecommended
⚠️ DPDP Act Penalty Schedule — Know What's at Stake
Penalties under Schedule I of the DPDP Act 2023 — levied by the Data Protection Board of India (DPDPB):
ViolationSectionMaximum Penalty
Failure to implement reasonable data security safeguards (leading to breach)§8(5)₹250 Crore
Failure to notify DPDPB and affected Data Principals of a data breach§8(6)₹200 Crore
Non-fulfilment of duties relating to children's data§9₹200 Crore
Non-compliance by Significant Data Fiduciary with special obligations§10₹150 Crore
Non-fulfilment of Data Principal rights (access, correction, erasure, grievance)§11–13₹50 Crore
Breach of any other provision of the Act or RulesGeneral₹50 Crore
Non-compliance with DPDPB orders§28₹50 Crore
* Penalties are per contravention and may accumulate. The DPDPB will assess quantum based on gravity, duration, type of data, and remedial action taken. Not legal advice — consult a qualified DPDP practitioner.
