Executive Summary

The regulatory confluence facing every PSU board

Two distinct mandates have converged. The DPDP Act 2023 makes every PSU a Data Fiduciary the moment it processes citizen personal data. The CAG IS Audit Manual 2024, anchored on COBIT 2019 and ISO/IEC 27001:2022, is the lens through which that processing — and the systems that enable it — will be examined. Boards that treat these as separate compliance programmes will pay twice and assure neither. The portal's PSU section is built around the single integrated control set that closes both.

The asymmetry that boards often miss: Section 17 of the DPDPA grants the state functional exemptions for welfare delivery, licensing and national security — but those carve-outs are narrow and non-institutional. The DPDPA also amends Section 8(1)(j) of the RTI Act 2005, removing the public-interest override and creating a statutory bar on disclosing personal information of public officials. PSUs must defend both fronts simultaneously: lawful processing under DPDPA, and reconfigured RTI response protocols. The Justice K.S. Puttaswamy judgment (2017, 9-judge bench) sits behind both as the constitutional baseline under Article 21.

Why most PSUs will be SDFs

Standard Data Fiduciary vs Significant Data Fiduciary — the gap that matters

Volume, sensitivity, sovereignty risk and use of emerging technologies. PSUs running citizen-facing databases will almost certainly be notified as SDFs under Section 10. The additional obligations are not incremental — they are the audit posture.

The Converged Roadmap

18-month PSU implementation — six phases

One roadmap. Both pillars. Each phase carries the documentation that satisfies both the CAG IS Audit Manual and the DPDPA Rules — designed so the same artefact answers both audits.

01
Months 1–3
Establish the Unified Governance Framework
Board resolution constituting the Data Governance Committee (chaired board-level; CISO, GC, CDO, IA representation). Section 17 Function Mapping Exercise to register every workflow as exempt or covered. Formal DPO appointment with direct reporting line to the Audit Committee. Published DPO contact across all citizen-facing channels.
Evidence: Board resolution · Section 17 Exemption Register · DPO publication
02
Months 4–6
Data Discovery and Inventory Mapping
Deploy automated discovery across SAP, Oracle, file systems, cloud. Classify against the 5-tier model with focus on Aadhaar, PAN, biometrics, banking credentials. Compile the master Record of Processing Activities (RoPA). Score every system on Sensitivity × Volume × Control-Gap; remediate above threshold.
Evidence: Data inventory · Classification policy · Signed RoPA · Risk scoring sheet
03
Months 7–9
SUD & SDLC Controls
For every new project: approved Project Initiation Document and Feasibility Study reviewed by the Data Governance Committee before development. Strict separation of dev / test / production. No real citizen data in non-production — masking or synthetic data only. Input validation and SQL hardening designed in, not bolted on.
Evidence: PI & FS checklists · SDLC security design · Environment separation report
04
Months 10–12
Consent & Rights Management Operationalisation
Re-engineer every collection point for granular, unbundled consent. No pre-ticked boxes, no bundled terms. In-house consent ledger or registered Consent Manager integration capturing principal ID, notice version, channel and consent state. Self-service Data Principal Rights Portal with 90-day SLA backend routing.
Evidence: Consent notices · Ledger schema · Rights Portal SLA register
05
Months 13–15
Technical Safeguards & Vendor Controls
AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 or higher in transit, full-disk encryption on endpoints, USB write-block on sensitive workstations. RBAC by least privilege, MFA enterprise-wide, tamper-proof log retention ≥ 12 months. Every processor contract upgraded with a binding DPA — purpose limitation, no unauthorised sub-processing, mandatory breach reporting, delete-on-termination.
Evidence: Encryption architecture · Endpoint reports · Executed DPAs · Supplier risk log
06
Months 16–18
Continuous Compliance & Performance Metrics
Automated Incident Response Playbook with 72-hour DPBI + individual notification path. Annual DPIA for every high-risk system. External independent data auditor engaged annually — opinion to Board and DPBI. COBIT-aligned CSF / KPI / KGI dashboard live, addressing the precise gap CAG flagged at BCPL.
Evidence: IR manual · DPIA reports · External audit certificate · KPI dashboard

CAG Audit Exhibit · Why this matters
CAG Report No. 2 of 2024 — Brahmaputra Cracker and Polymer Limited (BCPL)
The CAG's IS audit of selected CPSEs under the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas examined the SAP ERP implementation at BCPL and recorded three findings every PSU board should treat as a checklist: (i) failure to design or measure COBIT-aligned Critical Success Factors, KPIs and Key Goal Indicators; (ii) persistence of default usernames with default passwords in active database instances; (iii) assignment of multiple critical and conflicting authorisation roles to single users — a failure of segregation of duties. Under the DPDPA, the same control gaps are now penalty-bearing. A board that has not closed them carries both a CAG observation and a potential ₹250 crore exposure.

Next-Generation Governance

Rule 12 — Algorithmic Due Diligence for SDFs

A new obligation that catches every PSU deploying automated decision-making — from welfare eligibility scoring to traffic challan engines. SDFs must verify that any algorithmic system used to host, display, upload, modify, transmit or share personal data does not pose risks to the rights of data principals.

  • Explainability mechanism — every automated adverse decision must be capable of human-readable explanation
  • Bias detection — periodic testing on representative datasets, documented in the DPIA
  • Human-in-the-loop — meaningful review for decisions affecting entitlements, licensing or enforcement
  • Tamper-proof decision logs — sufficient to support an independent audit of any individual decision
  • Annual DPIA coverage — Rule 12 obligations integrated into the high-risk processing DPIA
