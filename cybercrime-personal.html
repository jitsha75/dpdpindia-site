Category 02 · Identity, Dignity & Harassment · 10 Crimes

Personal & Social Cybercrimes

Sextortion, SIM swap, deepfakes, cyberbullying, ransomware — crimes that target your identity, privacy, dignity, and safety. Every entry covers how it's done, your legal rights, and exact steps to get justice.

01
Sexual Exploitation · Extortion
Sextortion & Nude Video Call Scam
Also known as: "WhatsApp Video Call Blackmail", "Honey Trap Video Scam", "Obscene Video Fraud"
🔴 Extremely High Risk — Targets all genders
💜 If This Has Happened To You
You have done nothing wrong. This is a crime committed against you. Do not pay — payment always leads to more demands, never to the content being deleted. You are protected by law. Read the remedy section and take action today.
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
A stranger (profile picture of an attractive person) sends you a WhatsApp/Instagram friend request. After brief chatting, they initiate a video call and display nudity. They use screen recording software to record your face and reactions. Within minutes they send you the recording and demand ₹5,000–₹50,000 or they will share the video with your family/employer/contacts. They often already have your contact list from a data breach or social media scraping. In the "fake police call" variant, a person posing as a cyber police officer joins the call to increase pressure.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • Do not pay — it will not stop the blackmail
  • Block and report the person on the platform immediately
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in — "Cyber Blackmailing/Threatening"
  • File FIR at cyber police station — bring screenshots of the threat
  • Call Women's Helpline 1091 (if female victim)
  • Report the account to WhatsApp/Instagram — they take CSAM and sextortion seriously and will remove content
🛡️ Prevention
How to Stay Safe
  • Never accept video calls from unknown numbers
  • Cover your front camera when not in active use
  • Do not engage with strangers who initiate conversations on social media
  • Set your social media profiles to private and limit friend list visibility
Step-by-Step Recovery Procedure
1
DO NOT PAY
Block attacker on all platforms immediately
2

3

4

Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66E (Privacy Violation) · s.67 (Obscene electronic material) · s.67A (Sexually explicit content) · BNS 2023: s.74 (Assault/Criminal Force with intent to outrage modesty) · s.351 (Criminal Intimidation) · Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act 2012 — if victim is a minor
02
Identity Theft · Telecom Fraud
SIM Swap Fraud
Also known as: "SIM Cloning", "Mobile Number Hijacking", "Port Out Scam"
🔴 Very High Risk — Empties bank accounts silently
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
The criminal already has your name, Aadhaar number, and bank details (bought from data brokers or harvested from phishing). They visit a telecom retailer with a fake ID matching your name and request a new SIM for "lost phone." Once they get your number, your SIM stops working. They immediately use your mobile number to receive all OTPs and drain your bank accounts, transfer funds, and change banking passwords. You don't realise until your SIM goes dead and you check your balance.
⚖️ Your Remedy
Act the Moment Your SIM Goes Dead
  • Call your telecom operator immediately to block the duplicate SIM
  • Call your bank using another phone — freeze net banking and cards
  • Call 1930 for financial fraud reporting
  • Visit telecom store with ID proof to get SIM reactivated
  • File FIR + cybercrime.gov.in complaint
  • File TRAI complaint: trai.gov.in → Complaint Portal
🛡️ Prevention
Set Up These Guards Today
  • Enable SIM lock / SIM PIN on your phone settings
  • Register a secondary email for banking notifications — not just SMS
  • Enable two-factor authentication using an authenticator app, not SMS
  • If your SIM suddenly shows "No Network" — call operator immediately
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66C (Identity Theft) · s.43 (Unauthorized access) · BNS 2023: s.319 · Indian Wireless Telegraphy Act 1933 · Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act 1997 · RBI circular on unauthorized banking transactions
03
Identity Fraud · Document Misuse
Aadhaar / PAN Identity Theft
Also known as: "Document Misuse", "Fake Loan Using Aadhaar", "Ghost Account Fraud"
🔴 High Risk
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
Criminals use your Aadhaar number (obtained from data leaks, photocopies left at shops, or scam calls) to open bank accounts, take loans, get SIM cards, or register businesses in your name. In the eKYC variant, they use an OTP they trick you into sharing to complete eKYC authentication. You discover this months later when loan recovery agents contact you, or when CIBIL shows unknown loans, or when police contact you for crimes committed by someone using your identity.
⚖️ Your Remedy
Damage Control Steps
  • Lock Aadhaar biometrics immediately at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in
  • Call UIDAI helpline 1947 to report misuse
  • Check Aadhaar authentication history at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in
  • File FIR for identity theft with Aadhaar details
  • File complaint with RBI/bank if loan was taken
  • Check CIBIL report (free once/year) and raise dispute for unknown accounts
🛡️ Prevention
Protect Your Aadhaar Now
  • Lock your Aadhaar biometrics permanently — unlock only at a UIDAI centre when needed
  • Use Masked Aadhaar (shows only last 4 digits) for document submission
  • Write the purpose and date on any photocopy: "For [Bank Name] KYC only — [Date]"
  • Regularly check your Aadhaar authentication history
Applicable Law
Aadhaar Act 2016: s.29 (Prohibition on sharing) · s.40 (Penalty for impersonation) · IT Act 2000: s.66C (Identity Theft — up to 3 years imprisonment + ₹1 lakh fine) · BNS 2023: s.319
04
AI-Enabled Crime · Privacy Violation
Morphed Images & Deepfake Abuse
Also known as: "Fake Nude Photo Blackmail", "AI Deepfake Pornography", "Face Swap Abuse"
🔴 High Risk — Rising rapidly in 2024–26
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
Criminals download photos from social media profiles and use AI deepfake tools to morph faces onto explicit images or videos. These are used to blackmail the victim, shared on pornographic websites, sent to employers/family, or used as harassment in personal disputes. Some cases involve ex-partners creating revenge porn using deepfake tools. AI voice cloning is also being used — cloning a family member's voice to fake a kidnapping and demand ransom.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in — "Online Cyber Trafficking / CSAM / MMS" category
  • Use Google's "Remove non-consensual explicit images" tool: support.google.com/websearch
  • Report to each platform hosting the content (Meta, X, pornographic sites have takedown teams)
  • File FIR — mention "deepfake pornography" explicitly
  • Document all URLs with timestamps as evidence
🛡️ Prevention
Limit Your Digital Footprint
  • Set all social media photo albums to "Friends Only" or Private
  • Limit high-resolution public photos — profile picture does not need to be high-res
  • Google yourself periodically and set up Google Alerts for your name
  • Report suspicious accounts downloading or mirroring your content
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66E (Privacy Violation — up to 3 years + ₹2 lakh) · s.67 (Obscene material — up to 5 years) · s.67A (Sexually explicit content) · BNS 2023: s.74 · DPDP Act 2023 (unauthorized use of biometric/facial data) · IT Rules 2021: Rule 3(1)(b) — intermediaries must remove such content within 24 hours of notice
05
Online Harassment · Mental Harm
Cyberbullying & Online Harassment
Also known as: "Trolling", "Online Defamation", "Coordinated Harassment Campaign"
🟡 Medium-High Risk — Significant mental health impact
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
Cyberbullying includes sending repeated abusive messages, public humiliation on social media, creating fake profiles to impersonate, doxxing (publishing private information like home address), coordinated harassment campaigns where multiple accounts mass-report or abuse a target, sharing embarrassing or private content without consent, and making threats of physical violence. Workplace cyberbullying via email/Slack and school cyberbullying via class WhatsApp groups are increasingly reported.
⚖️ Your Remedy
Your Rights & Remedies
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in — "Cyber Bullying/Stalking/Sexting"
  • File FIR — you can file for criminal intimidation and defamation
  • Report fake profiles to the platform — platforms are required to act within 36 hours (IT Rules 2021)
  • If under 18: contact National Commission for Protection of Child Rights (NCPCR): ncpcr.gov.in
  • Seek an injunction from a civil court to stop harassment
🛡️ Prevention
Protect Yourself Online
  • Enable privacy settings — restrict who can comment on/share your posts
  • Document everything before blocking — screenshots with timestamps
  • Don't engage with trolls — report and block immediately
  • Talk to a trusted adult or counsellor — mental health support is available
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66A (struck down but conduct still punishable under BNS) · s.67 (obscene content) · BNS 2023: s.351 (Criminal Intimidation) · s.356 (Defamation) · s.78 (Stalking) · IT Rules 2021: Rule 3(1)(b) — platforms must address harassment complaints within 24 hours
06
Stalking · Surveillance Crime
Cyberstalking
Also known as: "Online Tracking", "Digital Surveillance", "GPS Monitoring Without Consent"
🔴 High Risk — Often precedes physical violence
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
Cyberstalking includes monitoring all social media activity, creating fake profiles to send friend requests and monitor your posts, tracking your location through shared location features, installing spyware (spy apps like mSpy, FlexiSPY) on victim's phone to read messages and track GPS, sending repeated unwanted messages, showing up at locations mentioned online, hacking into email/social accounts to read private messages.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • File FIR immediately — stalking is a cognizable offence under BNS s.78
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in — "Cyber Bullying/Stalking"
  • Women can file complaint at One Stop Centre (1800-11-0007)
  • Seek a Protection Order from a Magistrate under CrPC/BNSS
  • Factory-reset your phone to remove spyware; change all passwords
🛡️ Prevention
Digital Safety Steps
  • Review app permissions — revoke location access for all non-essential apps
  • Check phone for unfamiliar apps — spyware often hides as utility apps
  • Turn off "Location Sharing" on Google Maps/WhatsApp if you shared it previously
  • Use a different email and phone number for social media vs banking
Applicable Law
BNS 2023: s.78 (Stalking — up to 5 years for repeat offence) · IT Act 2000: s.66E (Privacy Violation) · s.43 (Unauthorized computer access — installing spyware) · Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act 2005 (if by intimate partner)
07
Social Engineering · Link-Based Fraud
Phishing & Smishing
Also known as: "Fake Website Link", "SMS Fraud Link", "Email Spoofing"
🔴 High Risk
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
Phishing uses fake emails; Smishing uses fake SMS. You receive a message looking exactly like it's from SBI, HDFC, IRDAI, Amazon, or UIDAI — with their logo, fonts, and formatting. The link takes you to a website that looks identical to the real one. You enter your login credentials or card details. The criminals harvest this and immediately access your account. Some links install malware automatically when clicked.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • Change all passwords immediately from a clean device
  • Call 1930 if financial loss occurred
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in
  • Report the phishing URL to MeitY at cybercrime.gov.in/report-phishing
  • Report the phishing email to Google/Microsoft spam reporting
🛡️ Prevention
URL Verification
  • Always check the URL carefully before entering credentials — fake sites use sbi-bank.com instead of sbi.co.in
  • Look for the padlock (HTTPS) — but note fake sites also have HTTPS now
  • Banks never send password reset links via SMS
  • Install a browser extension that detects phishing sites (Netcraft, Windows Defender)
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66 (Hacking/Computer fraud) · s.66C (Identity Theft) · s.66D (Cheating by impersonation) · s.43 (Unauthorized access) · BNS 2023: s.318 · TRAI Unsolicited Commercial Communication Regulations 2018
08
Malware · Data Extortion
Ransomware & Malware Attacks
Also known as: "File Encryption Extortion", "Crypto Locker", "Data Kidnapping"
🔴 High Risk for businesses and individuals
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
You click on a link or download an email attachment. Malware silently installs on your device. Ransomware encrypts all your files and shows a screen demanding payment in cryptocurrency to "unlock" them. Even if you pay, decryption is not guaranteed. Spyware steals banking credentials, browsing history, and saved passwords. For individuals, ransomware enters via pirated software, fake app downloads, or email attachments. For businesses, it enters via unpatched servers or phishing of employees.
⚖️ Your Remedy
Immediate Response
  • Disconnect from the internet immediately — prevent further encryption/exfiltration
  • Do not pay the ransom — no guarantee of recovery
  • Report to CERT-In: incident@cert-in.org.in or call 1800-11-4949
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in
  • File FIR — bring your device to the cyber police station
  • Check nomoreransom.org — free decryption tools for many ransomware families
🛡️ Prevention
Basic Cyber Hygiene
  • Keep regular offline backups (external hard drive not connected to internet)
  • Never open email attachments from unknown senders
  • Keep OS and antivirus updated — patch known vulnerabilities
  • Never use pirated software — it is the most common delivery mechanism
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.43 (Damage to computer — compensation) · s.66 (Computer-related offence — up to 3 years) · s.70 (Attacking protected systems — up to 10 years) · CERT-In Directions 2022 (mandatory 6-hour incident reporting for organisations) · BNS 2023: s.351 (Criminal Intimidation)
09
Social Engineering · Emotional Manipulation
Romance Scam / Honey Trap
Also known as: "Matrimonial Fraud", "Dating App Scam", "Fake NRI/Army Officer Fraud"
🔴 High Risk — Average loss: ₹3–15 Lakh
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
A person — often posing as an NRI, army officer, or doctor — contacts you on a matrimonial site, dating app, or social media. They invest weeks or months building an emotional relationship. Then an "emergency" happens: they're stuck at customs, there's a medical emergency, they need you to receive a parcel. They ask you to pay "clearance fees," send money via Western Union/crypto, or share your bank account to "transfer funds." Some variations involve sending you expensive fake gifts that you must pay customs to receive — there's no gift.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • Call 1930 if money was transferred
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in
  • File FIR — include all chat history, transaction details, profile screenshots
  • Report the profile to the platform and matrimonial site
  • Reverse Google Image Search the profile picture — scammers reuse stolen photos
🛡️ Prevention
Verify Before You Trust
  • Reverse image search all profile photos before engaging emotionally
  • Video call early — AI filters can't fully replicate real conversation yet
  • Never send money to anyone you haven't met in person
  • Share with a trusted friend or family member before making any financial decision
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66D (Cheating by impersonation using computer resource) · BNS 2023: s.318 (Cheating) · s.319 (Cheating by personation) · For army impersonation: s.140 BNS · Army Act 1950
10
Corporate Fraud · Email Spoofing
Business Email Compromise (BEC)
Also known as: "CEO Fraud", "Vendor Payment Fraud", "Invoice Redirect Scam"
🔴 Very High Risk for businesses — average loss: ₹15–50 Lakh
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
Criminals study a company's internal email structure. They spoof or hack the CEO's/CFO's email and send urgent payment instructions to the finance team: "Transfer ₹50 lakh to this new vendor account for a confidential deal — do it today and don't tell anyone." In the vendor variant, they intercept an ongoing procurement email thread and subtly change the bank account number in the invoice. The payment goes to the criminal's account. A single successful BEC can bankrupt an SME.
⚖️ Your Remedy
Recovery Steps
  • Call 1930 within minutes of realising
  • Call your bank — request SWIFT/NEFT transaction recall immediately
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in under "Business Email Compromise"
  • File FIR — specifically mention the spoofed email and transaction details
  • Report to CERT-In: incident@cert-in.org.in
🛡️ Prevention
Process Controls That Prevent BEC
  • Any payment above a threshold must be verified by phone call to a known number — not a number in the email
  • Verify bank account changes through a separate communication channel
  • Enable DMARC/SPF/DKIM on your company email domain
  • Train finance staff to verify unusual payment requests via phone, always
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66 (Computer fraud) · s.66C (Identity Theft via email spoofing) · s.66D (Cheating by impersonation) · BNS 2023: s.318 · CERT-In Directions 2022 (mandatory incident reporting for organisations) · Companies Act 2013: Board liability for cyber risk management
