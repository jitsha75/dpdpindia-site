DPDP Act & Rules — Implementation Watch
Supreme Court Feb – Mar 2026
Constitutional Challenge to DPDP Act Referred to Larger Bench — Hearings Ongoing in March 2026

A Bench comprising Chief Justice Surya Kant and Justices Joymalya Bagchi and V.M. Pancholi, hearing on 16 February 2026, refused to grant an interim stay on the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 and referred the constitutional challenge to a larger bench, with hearings scheduled for March 2026.

The petitions challenge Sections 17(1)(c), 17(2), 33(1), 36, and 44(3) of the DPDP Act 2023 and Rules 17 and 23(2) of the DPDP Rules 2025, alleging violations of Articles 14, 19(1)(a) and 21 of the Constitution. The challenge centres on broad government exemptions and adequacy of the consent framework, raising civil society concerns about surveillance potential.

Strategic Implication

Businesses in Phase 1 compliance should not pause implementation — the stay was refused. However, legal teams must track the larger bench proceedings closely, as any adverse ruling could materially alter obligations under Sections 17 and 33. A constitutional reading-down of government exemptions would significantly strengthen data principal rights.

Source: Internet Freedom Foundation — Supreme Court Observer
DPDP Phase 2 Countdown Active · Updated March 2026
233 Days to Consent Manager Framework Activation — Enterprises Must Prepare Now

With Phase 1 (Data Protection Board constituted, 13 November 2025) now operational, organisations must begin active preparation for Phase 2 activation on 13 November 2026, when the consent manager registration framework goes live. Consent Managers must demonstrate a minimum net worth of ₹2 crore, be incorporated in India, and meet independent certification requirements for platform interoperability.

Full substantive compliance — notice requirements, Data Principal rights, breach notification obligations, cross-border transfer restrictions, and children’s data processing rules — takes full effect on 13 May 2027. Penalties up to ₹250 crore per violation apply from that date.

Board-Level Action Points

CISOs and General Counsel should treat November 2026 as the hard internal deadline for consent management architecture. Outsourced consent manager vendors must be evaluated now — the ₹2 crore net-worth and certification requirements mean the qualified vendor pool will be limited and could face supply-side constraints.

Source: IAPP · Fisher Phillips LLP · SecurePrivacy

Court Rulings & Digital Rights
Supreme Court 13 March 2026
SC Clarifies RTI Cannot Override Personal Data Privacy — Legality-Necessity-Proportionality Test Mandatory

In a ruling dated 13 March 2026, the Supreme Court clarified that public officials retain privacy rights under Article 21, and that personal data cannot be disclosed under the Right to Information Act 2005 without satisfying a three-part test: legality, necessity, and proportionality — mirroring the framework established in Justice K.S. Puttaswamy v. Union of India (2017).

The ruling creates significant precedent at the intersection of the RTI Act and the emerging DPDP Act regime, reinforcing that no right — not even transparency — can operate as an absolute override of privacy.

Legal Significance

Central and State Public Information Officers must now apply this three-pronged test before disclosing personal data of officials in RTI responses. This ruling will directly influence the Data Protection Board’s adjudication framework once penalties go live in May 2027, particularly in cases involving government data fiduciaries.

Source: Legal Service India
Delhi High Court March 2026
Delhi HC Grants Interim Order Against AI Deepfakes — Personality Rights Upheld Under IT Act

The Delhi High Court granted an interim order protecting the personality rights of senior journalist Sudhir Chaudhary against AI-generated deepfake videos circulating on YouTube, directing the platform to take down allegedly misleading synthetic content. The order was passed invoking the IT Act’s intermediary liability framework alongside civil law remedies for personality rights.

This ruling signals India’s courts’ increasing willingness to apply existing legal tools to AI-generated harmful content while the legislature considers dedicated deepfake regulations.

Policy Watch

This ruling accelerates the case for dedicated deepfake or synthetic media regulation under a future IT Act amendment or DPDP subordinate legislation. In the interim, intermediaries face heightened liability under Sections 79 and 85 of the IT Act upon receiving notice of deepfake content — platforms should update their take-down SLAs accordingly.

Source: SCC Online

Government & Digital Public Infrastructure
E-Governance, MeitY & UIDAI Updates
MeitY Initiative 23 February 2026
MeitY Launches Blockchain India Challenge 2026 — Targets Land Records, PDS, Healthcare & Document Verification

Inaugurated by MeitY Secretary S. Krishnan on 23 February 2026, the Blockchain India Challenge 2026 invites Indian startups to develop permissioned-blockchain solutions for digital governance. Priority use cases include land records (integration with DILRMP), Public Distribution System, healthcare records, and tamper-proof document verification. The initiative aligns with Digital India 3.0’s goal of immutable, transparent public service delivery.

Separately, MeitY has constituted a high-level committee for Harmonisation of the Regulatory Framework for DigiLocker, with terms of reference including legislative review of Digital Locker Rules and a future roadmap for Entity Locker expansion.

Opportunity & Compliance Note

Solutions deployed under this programme handling personal data must build DPDP Act compliance in from day one — particularly consent management and data minimisation obligations under the DPDP Rules 2025. DPDP-readiness will become a key evaluation criterion for government procurement of data-processing technology.

Source: EduNovations · MeitY.gov.in
UIDAI / Aadhaar Annual Report FY 2024–25
Aadhaar Authentication Surpasses 2,707 Crore Transactions in FY25 — New Governance Portal Launched

UIDAI confirmed that Aadhaar authentication crossed 2,707 crore transactions in FY 2024–25, with eKYC alone approaching 45 crore transactions in the month of March. MeitY also launched a new Aadhaar Governance Portal to streamline the inclusion of new use cases in public service delivery, following amendments to the Aadhaar Authentication for Good Governance (Social Welfare, Innovation, Knowledge) Rules 2025.

UIDAI Circular 7 of 2025 updated the eKYC XML and PDF response format for foreign nationals enrolled as Aadhaar holders — a significant operational update for banks, telecom operators, and fintech companies serving NRI customers.

Source: UIDAI Press Release · DD News

Domestic Cyber Threats
India Cybercrime & CERT-In
CERT-In Advisory 19 March 2026
CERT-In CIVN-2026-0150: Critical RCE Vulnerabilities Across Apple Ecosystem — Patch Immediately

CERT-In issued advisory CIVN-2026-0150 classifying multiple remote-code-execution (RCE) vulnerabilities across Apple iOS, macOS, iPadOS, watchOS, and Safari as critically important. Exploiting these flaws allows attackers to execute unauthorised code remotely on unpatched devices without user interaction in certain attack vectors.

The advisory urgently directs all individual consumers, enterprise IT teams, and government departments to install the latest security patches via device software update settings.

Compliance Action Required

Under CERT-In’s April 2022 cybersecurity directions, regulated entities must report incidents within 6 hours. A known-unpatched device that is subsequently exploited would constitute gross negligence. CISO teams should document patch deployment timelines as evidence of due diligence for any future Data Protection Board inquiry.

Source: CERT-In Advisories · Prameya News
Threat Intelligence Seqrite Report · March 2026
Cybercrime Up 24% in FY25: ₹22,495 Cr Lost — Healthcare & Govt Top Ransomware Targets in 2026

The Seqrite India Cyber Threat Report 2026, drawing on Ministry of Home Affairs data, confirms a 24% spike in cybercrime with total FY25 losses of ₹22,495 crore. Investment scams alone accounted for over 75% of financial losses. Healthcare, Education, and Manufacturing together constituted nearly 47% of all malware detections (Oct 2024 – Sep 2025).

Ransomware surged 31% above prior nine-month averages in January 2026 alone, with healthcare leading at 27 incidents per month, followed by government (11) and manufacturing (10). India accounts for approximately 3% of global ransomware victims. A coordinated hybrid warfare campaign by APT36 and SideCopy threat actors was documented as having infiltrated Indian defence and government networks during this period.

Policy & Regulatory Implication

The APT36/SideCopy campaign targeting defence networks adds urgency to India’s Space Cybersecurity Guidelines 2026 (CERT-In/SIA-India) and raises questions about mandatory incident reporting timelines. Healthcare entities must urgently review the 6-hour reporting obligation under CERT-In 2022 directions. The ₹22,495 crore loss figure should directly inform the Data Protection Board’s penalty calibration discussions ahead of May 2027.

Source: Seqrite Threat Report 2026 · Insights on India · The Cyber Express

International Cybersecurity Incidents — India Relevance
Global Breach 12 March 2026
Crunchyroll Breach: 6.8 Million Users’ Data Stolen via BPO Okta SSO Compromise

Anime streaming platform Crunchyroll (Sony Pictures) confirmed investigation of a breach affecting approximately 6.8 million users after threat actors compromised the Okta Single Sign-On (SSO) account of a Telus International BPO support agent. The intrusion, claimed on 12 March 2026, exposed user email addresses, subscription data, and viewing history.

The breach is the latest in a series of Okta supply-chain compromises and highlights the critical vulnerability of outsourced support operations as a privileged entry vector into first-party systems.

India BPO Sector Alert

India’s large IT-BPO industry must note: where Indian BPO support agents access data of Indian users on behalf of a foreign principal, the Indian entity may constitute a Data Fiduciary or Data Processor under the DPDP Act. The Crunchyroll breach model — outsourced support with privileged SSO access — is endemic in Indian BPO operations. Boards must review third-party access control policies as a DPDP Phase 2 priority.

Source: BleepingComputer
UK Government Breach 16–20 March 2026
UK Companies House WebFiling Flaw Exposes Personal Data of 5 Million Company Directors

A security vulnerability in the UK Government’s Companies House WebFiling portal, disclosed around 16–20 March 2026, exposed personal data — including email addresses, home addresses, and dates of birth of company directors — for approximately 5 million registered companies. The breach highlights systemic risks in legacy government digital service infrastructure worldwide.

India Parallel: MCA21

India’s MCA21 portal (Ministry of Corporate Affairs) carries structurally analogous risks as it stores personal data of directors and signatories. With DPDP Rules mandating breach notification and the DigiLocker Harmonisation Committee now active, MCA and NIC should conduct urgent security audits of all public registries containing personal data. The UK incident provides a concrete risk benchmark for the DPDP Board’s future guidance on government data fiduciaries.

Source: UpGuard
Supply Chain Attack 19 March 2026
Checkmarx GitHub Actions Compromised by TeamPCP — Credential-Stealing Malware in DevSecOps Pipeline

Two GitHub Actions CI/CD workflows maintained by supply-chain security vendor Checkmarx were compromised by threat actor TeamPCP via credential-stealing malware, discovered approximately 19 March 2026. The attack targeted a security vendor’s own automated build pipelines, demonstrating the “hack the defender” supply-chain vector that can cascade silently to thousands of downstream software projects.

India DevSecOps Alert

Indian software exporters, SaaS companies, and government IT vendors using GitHub Actions should immediately audit all third-party action dependencies. Given India’s CERT-In 2022 directions on supply-chain security and the upcoming MeitY Cloud Policy revision, this incident is likely to accelerate mandatory CI/CD security standards for software suppliers to government.
