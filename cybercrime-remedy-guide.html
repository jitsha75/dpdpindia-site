Complete Step-by-Step Handbook

How to Report a Cybercrime & Get Justice in India

Exact steps for every channel: the 1930 helpline, cybercrime.gov.in portal, FIR at cyber police station, bank reversal process, RBI Ombudsman, and what to do if police refuse your complaint.

⚡ The Most Important Thing You Will Read
If Your Money Was Stolen — Act Now, Not Later
Cyber police can freeze the destination bank account and trigger a reversal if notified within 1–2 hours. After 4 hours, criminals withdraw via ATM or transfer further. After 24 hours, the trail is cold. Every minute you wait reduces your recovery chances dramatically. Call first. File paperwork after.

The Complete Process

The 5-Step Cybercrime Complaint Process

Follow these steps in order. For financial fraud, Steps 1 and 2 are urgent and must be done immediately. Steps 3–5 can follow within 24–72 hours.

1
Call 1930 — The National Cyber Crime Helpline
⚡ Do This First — Within 60 Minutes

Who answers: A trained cyber crime operator at your State Cyber Cell. The call is free from any mobile or landline in India.

What to keep ready before calling:

  • The fraudulent transaction ID / UTR number (from your bank SMS)
  • Exact amount lost and the time of transaction
  • The UPI ID, phone number, or bank account the money went to
  • Your own bank name and account number

What happens when you call: The operator creates a complaint and flags the destination account with the destination bank for freezing. They give you a complaint number — save it. This complaint number is required for all subsequent steps.

For non-financial crimes (sextortion, cyberbullying, stalking), still call 1930 — they will guide you to the correct next step.

2
Call Your Bank — Request Transaction Freeze
⚡ Urgent — Within 2 Hours

Immediately after calling 1930, call your bank's 24×7 customer care (number on the back of your debit card). Say: "Unauthorised transaction — I need to raise a chargeback and freeze my account."

  • Request them to mark the transaction as "disputed / unauthorised"
  • Ask for a complaint reference number from the bank
  • Ask the bank to coordinate with the beneficiary bank under the 1930 freeze request
  • Follow up within 24 hours in writing via email to your bank's nodal officer

RBI Rule: Under the RBI Circular on Limiting Customer Liability in Unauthorised Electronic Transactions (2017), if you report within 3 working days of getting the bank's fraud notification, your maximum liability is zero (for third-party fraud with no negligence on your part). Report in writing immediately.

3
File Online at cybercrime.gov.in
Within 24 Hours

The National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal at cybercrime.gov.in is operated by MHA. Filing here creates a formal record that police stations across India are required to act on.

Step-by-step on the portal:

  • Go to cybercrime.gov.in → Click "Report Cyber Crime"
  • Select category: Financial Fraud / Other Cyber Crime / Women & Children (as applicable)
  • Click "Report Financial Fraud" for money-related crimes
  • Enter: your details, fraud type, amount, transaction ID, fraudster's phone/account
  • Upload screenshots as evidence (transaction SMS, chat screenshots, fraud website screenshots)
  • Submit → You receive an Acknowledgement Number — save it
  • The complaint auto-routes to your state's Cyber Cell for follow-up

For Women & Children crimes (CSAM, trafficking, obscene content): use the separate "Report & Track" section — these get priority processing and are handled by dedicated units.

4
File an FIR at the Cyber Police Station
Within 48–72 Hours

The online portal complaint is not an FIR. For serious financial fraud, identity theft, sextortion, or any crime requiring arrest or asset freezing, you need a formal First Information Report (FIR).

Where to go: Your city's dedicated Cyber Crime Police Station. Most major cities now have a standalone Cyber Cell. If unavailable, any police station can register an FIR — they are legally required to under the BNSS 2023 (formerly CrPC).

What to bring:

  • Printouts of transaction history from your bank app/netbanking
  • Screenshots of all chats, calls, and messages from the fraudster
  • The 1930 complaint number and cybercrime.gov.in acknowledgement number
  • Your Aadhaar and photo ID for the complainant section
  • A written complaint narrative (1 page explaining what happened)

At the station: Ask for the "Cyber Crime Cell" officer. Give them the complaint number from 1930 — this speeds up the process significantly. Insist on receiving a copy of the FIR with a date stamp. You are legally entitled to a copy.

5
Escalate If Bank Doesn't Cooperate — RBI Banking Ombudsman
If Bank Refuses Refund After 30 Days

If your bank refuses to process the refund or does not respond within 30 days of your written complaint, file with the RBI Integrated Ombudsman Scheme.

  • File at: bankingombudsman.rbi.org.in
  • Or call: 14448 (RBI Helpline)
  • You must have first complained to the bank in writing and waited 30 days (or received an unsatisfactory reply)
  • Attach: your original complaint to the bank, their response (or proof of no response), FIR copy, transaction details
  • RBI Ombudsman decisions are binding on banks — they can order full refund plus compensation

Preserve Your Case

How to Collect & Preserve Digital Evidence

Strong evidence significantly increases your chances of recovery and prosecution. The golden rule: screenshot everything before blocking or deleting.

Evidence Checklist — Collect Before You Block
  • ☐
    Screenshots of all messages, chats, and call logs from the fraudster (include timestamps)
  • ☐
    Bank transaction SMS and app screenshots showing the fraudulent debit with UTR/transaction ID
  • ☐
    The fraudster's phone number, UPI ID, bank account number, or email address
  • ☐
    URLs of fake websites, fake apps, or spoofed emails (screenshot the full URL bar)
  • ☐
    Social media profile screenshots of the fraudster (before they deactivate the account)
  • ☐
    Screenshots of any fraudulent QR codes, payment requests, or links you received
  • ☐
    Any documents they sent (fake police orders, fake bank letters, fake lottery certificates)
  • ☐
    Your call log showing when you called the fraudster's number (for time evidence)
  • ☐
    Screen recordings if you can — more detailed than screenshots

How to preserve screenshots properly: Take a screenshot, then email it to yourself immediately. This creates a timestamped copy that cannot be claimed to be fabricated. If printing, get them notarised if possible. For videos or long chats, record a screen video of you scrolling through the evidence.

Your Rights

What to Do if Police Refuse to Register Your FIR

Police are legally required to register an FIR for any cognizable offence. Refusing to do so is itself an offence. If this happens:

Step 1 — Demand a written refusal
Ask the officer to provide in writing the reason for refusing to register the FIR. They will almost always back down at this stage — most refusals are informal. If they provide written refusal, this itself becomes evidence for the next steps.
Step 2 — Send complaint to SP / Commissioner by post with acknowledgement
Under BNSS 2023 (formerly CrPC s.154(3)), if a police station refuses to register an FIR, you can send your complaint by registered post to the Superintendent of Police (SP) or Commissioner of Police of the district. They are required to investigate and direct registration of the FIR. Send with speed post so you have proof of delivery.
Step 3 — File a private complaint before the Magistrate (BNSS 2023, s.223)
If the SP doesn't act within 15 days, file a private complaint (also called a "complaint case") directly before the Judicial Magistrate First Class (JMFC) of your area. The Magistrate can order the police to investigate. This route has been used successfully in many cyber fraud cases. A legal aid lawyer can assist you with this process at minimal cost.
Step 4 — Approach the State Human Rights Commission or High Court
Refusal to register an FIR for a cognizable offence is a violation of your fundamental rights. You can file a writ petition under Article 226 before your State High Court. Legal aid services under the National Legal Services Authority (NALSA) at nalsa.gov.in or toll-free 15100 can provide free legal assistance.
