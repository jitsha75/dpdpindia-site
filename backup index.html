Legislative History

India's Digital Law Timeline

From UNCITRAL's 1996 Model Law to the DPDP Act 2023 — India's digital legal framework has evolved over three decades of legislative milestones and landmark judicial decisions.

1996
UNCITRAL Model Law on E-Commerce
UN General Assembly Resolution 51/162 — the "functional equivalence" principle that inspired the IT Act 2000.
2000
IT Act 2000 Enacted & Notified
India's first dedicated cyber law — enacted May 2000, notified 17 October 2000. Established legal recognition for electronic records and the CCA.
2008–09
IT Amendment Act — Security Pivot
Section 43A (SPDI liability), Section 66A (later struck down), Section 69 (surveillance expansion), technology-neutral electronic signatures.
2015
Shreya Singhal v. Union of India
Supreme Court strikes down Section 66A; reads down Section 79 — intermediaries act only on court/government orders, not private complaints.
2016
Aadhaar Act — UIDAI Statutory Basis
Aadhaar authentication given statutory footing. eKYC framework established for financial inclusion and welfare delivery.
2017
Justice Puttaswamy — Right to Privacy
Nine-judge Constitution Bench unanimously holds privacy is a fundamental right under Article 21 — the foundation of the DPDP Act.
2021
IT Rules 2021 — Intermediary Guidelines
Comprehensive overhaul — due diligence, grievance officers, SSMIs, digital news framework under Parts I–III.
2023
DPDP Act 2023 — India's Privacy Law
Digital Personal Data Protection Act enacted August 2023. Implementing Rules pending gazette notification. Amends IT Act s. 43A and RTI Act s. 8.
