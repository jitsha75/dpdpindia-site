About

DPDP India is an independent policy research initiative examining the intersection of data protection, cybersecurity, digital governance, and technology policy.

As India rapidly digitizes public services, finance, and governance systems, issues related to data privacy, security, and responsible digital innovation have become central to public policy.

This initiative aims to contribute to informed discussion through research, analysis, and public engagement.

Research Focus

  • Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act)
  • Data privacy and citizen rights
  • Cybersecurity and data security policy
  • Digital governance and regulatory frameworks
  • Digital public infrastructure and identity systems
  • Technology policy and responsible innovation

DPDP Policy Timeline

2017

Supreme Court recognizes privacy as a fundamental right in the Puttaswamy judgment.

2018

Justice B.N. Srikrishna Committee proposes India’s first comprehensive data protection framework.

2019

Personal Data Protection Bill introduced in Parliament.

2022

Government withdraws the earlier bill to create a simplified digital data protection framework.

2023

Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act) passed by Parliament.
