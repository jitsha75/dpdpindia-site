About the Initiative

India's Most Focused
Data Governance
Research Platform

DPDP India is an independent policy and legal research initiative examining the architecture of data protection, digital governance, and regulatory frameworks in India at a critical juncture of legislative implementation.

At a time when the DPDP Act 2023 is being operationalised — with rules under consultation and the Data Protection Board yet to be constituted — this platform provides rigorous, independent analysis drawn from doctrinal legal scholarship and comparative policy research.

  • 01 Legal Doctrinal Research — Systematic statutory analysis of the DPDP Act, its rules, and the emerging jurisprudence on informational privacy under the Constitution.
  • 02 Comparative Policy Analysis — Benchmarking India's data protection regime against GDPR, PIPL, APEC Privacy Framework, and other global instruments.
  • 03 Regulatory Monitoring — Tracking MeitY consultations, TRAI recommendations, parliamentary developments, and stakeholder positions in real time.
  • 04 Public Policy Commentary — Accessible, evidence-based writing on emerging issues in AI governance, digital public infrastructure, and data sovereignty.

Legislative History

India's Data Protection
Journey, 2017 – Present

Seven years of constitutional litigation, parliamentary deliberation, and regulatory consultation that culminated in one of the world's most significant data governance frameworks.

2017 Aug
Privacy as Fundamental Right — Puttaswamy
The Supreme Court's nine-judge constitution bench unanimously held the right to privacy to be a fundamental right intrinsic to life and liberty under Article 21. The judgment set the doctrinal foundation for data protection legislation.
2018 Jul
Srikrishna Committee Report
Justice B.N. Srikrishna's expert committee submitted its landmark report with a draft Personal Data Protection Bill, proposing a comprehensive data protection framework modelled in part on GDPR with India-specific adaptations.
2019 Dec
Personal Data Protection Bill Introduced
The government introduced the PDP Bill in the Lok Sabha. The Bill was referred to a Joint Parliamentary Committee (JPC) for scrutiny, reflecting the complexity and breadth of the proposed legislation.
2021 Dec
JPC Report — 81 Amendments
The Joint Parliamentary Committee submitted its report recommending 81 amendments and broader non-personal data regulation scope. The report generated significant debate on government exemptions and data localisation provisions.
2022 Aug
PDP Bill Withdrawn
The government withdrew the 2019 Bill, citing the need for a comprehensive legal framework covering the broader digital ecosystem, signalling a fresh legislative approach.
2023 Aug
DPDP Act Enacted ★
The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 received Presidential assent. The Act establishes rights of data principals, obligations of data fiduciaries, and provides for the Data Protection Board of India as the adjudicatory authority.
2025 Ongoing
Rules Drafting & Stakeholder Consultation
Draft DPDP Rules under active public consultation. Key issues under deliberation: Consent Manager framework, significant data fiduciary designation criteria, cross-border data transfer mechanisms, and children's data protections.

Methodology

A Rigorous,
Independent Research Approach

Every analysis is grounded in primary legal sources, comparative frameworks, and direct engagement with regulatory developments.

01 / Doctrinal Legal Analysis
Primary Source Scholarship

Systematic examination of statutes, subordinate legislation, judicial pronouncements, and regulatory guidance to construct accurate doctrinal maps of India's data governance architecture. Every claim is sourced from primary legal texts.

02 / Comparative Research
Global Benchmarking

India's regulatory choices are benchmarked against GDPR, China's PIPL, APEC Privacy Framework, Brazil's LGPD, and other significant instruments to contextualise domestic developments within international best practice and divergences.

03 / Regulatory Monitoring
Continuous Policy Tracking

Active monitoring of MeitY consultations, TRAI recommendations, parliamentary committee proceedings, CERT-In directives, and civil society positions — providing timely, contextualised policy intelligence as events unfold.

04 / Public Commentary
Accessible Expert Writing

Translating complex legal analysis into readable policy commentary on blog.dpdpindia.in — written for lawyers, policymakers, technologists, and engaged citizens who need to understand India's digital governance landscape.
