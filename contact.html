How Your Information Is Used

Information you submit through this form is used solely to respond to your enquiry. It is not shared with any third party, used for marketing communications, or stored in any publicly accessible system.

DPDP India is an independent research initiative committed to the very principles it studies — including the responsible handling of personal data.

Data Handling Principles

In the spirit of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023, this form collects only the information necessary to process your enquiry (data minimisation). Your contact details are never sold, rented, or shared. You may request deletion of your information at any time by writing in.

This site does not use tracking cookies or third-party analytics scripts on the contact page.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is DPDP India affiliated with the government or any regulatory body?
No. DPDP India is a fully independent research initiative. It has no formal affiliation with the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY), the Data Protection Board of India, or any government body. All research and commentary represents the independent views of the researcher.
Can I cite research or articles from DPDP India in my own work?
Yes, research published on dpdpindia.in and blog.dpdpindia.in may be cited with appropriate attribution. Please use the full URL, author name (Jitendra Sharma), and publication date. For academic citations, please use the standard citation format applicable to your discipline.
Do you offer legal advice or compliance services?
DPDP India is a policy and legal research platform — it does not provide legal advice, legal opinions, or compliance consulting services. Content published here is for informational and academic purposes only and should not be relied upon as legal advice. For compliance needs, please consult a qualified legal practitioner.
I have a correction or factual update to share. How should I submit it?
Corrections and factual updates are welcome and taken seriously. Please use the contact form and select "Policy Feedback on Published Research" as the enquiry type. Include the specific article URL, the passage in question, and your suggested correction with supporting references.
Can students or researchers request an informational interview?
Requests from students and academic researchers are welcomed where time permits. Please use the contact form, select "Academic / Student Enquiry", describe your research question or thesis topic, and indicate your institutional affiliation. Requests are reviewed on a case-by-case basis.
How can I stay updated on new research and commentary?
The best way to stay current is to regularly visit blog.dpdpindia.in for policy commentary and analysis, and dpdpindia.in for research updates. You can also connect with Jitendra Sharma on LinkedIn to see new publications in your professional feed.
