Geospatial Data Under the DPDP Act: When the Map Becomes the Risk

With the DPDP Act enacted and phased enforcement underway, can governments still manage geospatial data the way they always have?

Government geospatial platforms have long been built on a foundational belief: maps and spatial data are technical public assets. Drone surveys, dashcam analytics, CCTV feeds, and GIS layers held by departments without mature information security frameworks — under the DPDP Act and IT Act, this decentralised model now creates systemic legal risk. Who is accountable when that data enables personal or inferable identification? The answer demands a platform-level governance rethink.
