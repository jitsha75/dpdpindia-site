Legislative Genesis — From Puttaswamy to the DPDP Act 2023
The constitutional and legislative journey that culminated in India's first comprehensive data protection law — from the 2017 Supreme Court right-to-privacy ruling through the Srikrishna Committee, five years of JPC deliberation, the withdrawal of the 2019 Bill, and the final enactment of the DPDP Act 2023. Essential context for understanding why the Act is designed the way it is.

The DPDP Act — Core Framework & Key Sections
A structured walkthrough of the DPDP Act's operative provisions — the SARAL framework, Section 4-6 notice and consent obligations, Section 8 Data Fiduciary duties, Section 9 children's data protections, and Section 10 Significant Data Fiduciary requirements including mandatory DPO appointment, DPIAs, and periodic audits. The foundational reading for compliance professionals.

Data Principal Rights — Sections 11–14 of the DPDP Act
A comprehensive analysis of the four rights granted to data principals under the Act — the right to access information about processing (Section 11), the right to correction and erasure (Section 12), the right to grievance redressal (Section 13), and the right to nominate a representative in case of death or incapacity (Section 14). Includes practical guidance on how organisations must implement these rights, timelines, and limitations.
Sec 11 — Right to access info Sec 12 — Correction & erasure Sec 13 — Grievance redressal Sec 14 — Right to nominate Response timelines Consent withdrawal Right to erasure limits

Rules & Implementation — DPDP Rules 2025
The procedural rules notified in 2025 that bridge the DPDP Act's principles and practical compliance. Covers the Consent Manager framework (third-party consent token platforms), the standardised breach notification protocol with prescribed timelines, the 18-month phased onboarding roadmap for legacy data, data localisation considerations, and MeitY's implementation guidance. Essential reading for compliance teams.

Adjudication & Penalties — The Data Protection Board
How the DPDP Act is enforced — the structure and powers of the Data Protection Board of India (DPB), the full dispute resolution flow from breach notification through adjudicatory order to TDSAT appeal, the fixed-cap penalty schedule (₹250 Crore maximum for security safeguard failures), and the Act's departure from GDPR's percentage-of-turnover model. Critical reading for General Counsel and risk teams.

Article 06 Exemptions & Controversy New Addition ~20 min read
Read article
Exemptions & Government Powers — Section 17 Analysis
A critical analysis of the DPDP Act's most contested provisions — Section 17's broad government exemptions, which allow the Central Government and its instrumentalities to be exempt from significant portions of the Act on grounds of national security, sovereignty, and public order. Covers the academic and civil society critique of these provisions, comparisons with GDPR's government exemption approach, and what the exemptions mean practically for citizens' data rights when the state is the data fiduciary.
Section 17 exemptions National security carve-out State as data fiduciary Civil society critique Puttaswamy proportionality Public order grounds GDPR Art 23 comparison
Article 07 Comparative Law Advanced ~22 min read
Read article
Global Privacy Jurisdictions — GDPR, US, DIFC & Beyond
A structured comparative analysis of the world's leading privacy frameworks — the EU GDPR's dignity-first model with percentage-of-turnover penalties, the US sectoral approach (HIPAA, GLBA) and California CCPA, the Dubai DIFC Law No. 5 of 2020 as a MEASA-to-West compliance corridor, and China's PIPL. Maps where India's DPDP Act converges with and deliberately diverges from these global benchmarks, and what this means for cross-border data flows affecting Indian businesses.
GDPR (EU) — Art 6 lawful bases GDPR % turnover penalties US HIPAA / GLBA sectoral California CCPA / CPRA DIFC Law No.5 of 2020 China PIPL Cross-border data flows India vs GDPR divergences
