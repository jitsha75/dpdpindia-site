National Cyber Crime Helpline — India

Intelligence Alerts

Latest Fraud Alerts — April 2026

Current active scam patterns reported across India. Updated regularly from CERT-In, cybercrime.gov.in, and verified media sources.

🔴 Critical — Active
Digital Arrest Scam — Fake CBI/ED Officers
How it works
Fraudsters pose as CBI, ED, TRAI, or police officers via video call. They claim your Aadhaar/phone is linked to drug trafficking or money laundering and demand immediate money transfer to "clear your name."
  • Unexpected video call from "official" number
  • Demands "stay on camera" for hours
  • Asks you to keep it secret from family
  • Requests immediate UPI transfer for "bail"
Updated: April 2026 Full Details →
🔴 Critical — Surge
Investment Fraud — Fake Trading Apps
How it works
Victims are added to WhatsApp "investment groups" where paid actors share fake profits. Victims invest via counterfeit trading apps showing false returns. Withdrawal is blocked or requires "taxes" to release funds — which are stolen.
  • WhatsApp group with "investment experts"
  • Shows high returns in custom app only
  • Withdrawal requires paying a "tax" first
  • App not on Google Play Store or Apple Store
Updated: March 2026 Full Details →
🟠 High — Active
AI Voice Cloning — Family Emergency Scam
How it works
Using AI, scammers clone a family member's voice from social media. They call pretending to be in a hospital/accident emergency and urgently request money transfer before you can verify.
  • Urgent call from "family member" in distress
  • Voice sounds slightly robotic or flat
  • Requests immediate UPI/bank transfer
  • Asks you not to call back on their number
Updated: April 2026 Full Details →
🟠 High — Rising
Fake Job Offers — Task-Based Fraud
How it works
Victims are offered work-from-home "like videos / complete tasks" jobs. Initial small payments build trust. Then larger "deposits" are required to unlock higher earnings — all funds are stolen.
  • Telegram-based "job" group
  • Initial small payment to build trust
  • Asks to invest increasing amounts
  • Work dashboard shows earnings that can't be withdrawn
Updated: March 2026 Full Details →
🟠 High — Ongoing
OTP / SIM Swap Fraud — Banking Takeover
How it works
Scammers use your Aadhaar/PAN details (bought from data breaches) to get a duplicate SIM from your carrier. They then reset banking passwords and drain accounts before you realise.
  • Your phone suddenly loses network
  • You stop receiving SMS for 30+ minutes
  • Unknown transaction OTPs received
  • Bank account access suddenly fails
Updated: February 2026 Full Details →
🟡 Medium — Seasonal
Income Tax Refund Fraud
How it works
Fraudsters send SMS/WhatsApp claiming your income tax refund is pending. They ask you to click a link and enter banking details to "receive" the refund — which instead drains your account.
  • SMS from unofficial number about "tax refund"
  • Link redirects to a fake IT portal
  • Asks for full banking credentials
  • Real IT refunds come via ECS — no link needed

Take Action

If It Happened to You — Act Now

Speed is critical. Every minute matters in financial fraud — banks can freeze transactions if you report within the golden window. Follow these steps in exact order.

01
Call 1930 Immediately
National Cyber Crime Helpline. Tell them: amount, bank, UPI ID or account number, and time of fraud. They will create a ticket and alert the bank's nodal officer.
⏱ Do this within 1 hour for best chance of recovery
02
Block Your Account & UPI
Immediately call your bank's 24×7 helpline (printed on your card) to freeze transactions. Open your UPI app and disable the bank account linked to it.
⏱ Do this simultaneously with calling 1930
03
File Online Complaint
Go to cybercrime.gov.in → "Report Cyber Crime" → "Financial Fraud". Attach screenshots, transaction IDs, and call logs. Get your complaint reference number.
⏱ Within 24 hours of the fraud
04
File an FIR
Visit your local police station or file on the e-FIR portal of your state. Take your cybercrime.gov.in complaint reference number. An FIR strengthens your recovery claim with the bank.
⏱ Within 72 hours

Prevention

Protect Yourself

Simple practices that dramatically reduce your exposure to the most common fraud types.

🏦
Banking Safety
  • Never share OTP, PIN, or CVV with anyone — banks never ask
  • Enable transaction alerts on WhatsApp and SMS
  • Set a daily UPI transaction limit in your bank app
  • Check bank account at least weekly for unknown transactions
  • Never use banking apps on public Wi-Fi
📞
Call & Video Safety
  • No real government officer ever demands money over phone/video
  • If threatened with "digital arrest" — hang up, call family
  • Verify caller identity by calling the official number independently
  • AI can clone voices — always have a family code word for emergencies
  • Screen-share = fraud risk; never share screen during a financial call
🌐
Online & Social Safety
  • Enable 2-factor authentication on all social media accounts
  • Investment groups on WhatsApp/Telegram are almost always frauds
  • Verify job offers on the company's official website directly
  • Never download APKs or apps from WhatsApp or Telegram links
  • Limit personal information visible on your social media profiles
🪪
Aadhaar & Identity Safety
  • Use Masked Aadhaar for KYC — shows last 4 digits only
  • Check Aadhaar authentication history on UIDAI.gov.in regularly
  • Lock your Aadhaar biometric when not in use (mAadhaar app)
  • Never share full Aadhaar photocopy for hotel check-ins etc.
  • Report suspicious Aadhaar use at 1947 or uidai.gov.in
📱
Phone & SIM Safety
  • If phone suddenly loses network — call carrier immediately (SIM Swap alert)
  • Set a SIM PIN through your phone settings
  • Keep telecom account linked to a separate secure email
  • Don't accept unknown call forwarding requests
  • Register your number on DND (trai.gov.in) to reduce fraud calls
👨‍👩‍👧
Protect Your Family
  • Brief elderly family members — they are the most targeted
  • Create a family "verification code" for emergency calls
  • Show elders how "digital arrest" scams work with examples
  • Set up Google Family Safety or parental controls for children
  • Know each other's bank — so you can help freeze accounts
