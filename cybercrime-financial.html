Category 01 · Most Common · 10 Crimes

Financial Cyber Frauds —
How They Work & How to Fight Back

10 financial cybercrimes explained: how criminals execute them, the exact laws that protect you, step-by-step remedy procedures, and how to never fall for them again.

01
Financial Fraud · Most Reported
UPI / QR Code Fraud
Also known as: "Collect Request Scam", "QR Scan Scam", "Money Request Fraud"
🔴 Very High Risk
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
Criminals pose as buyers on OLX/Facebook Marketplace or as "army officers" wanting to pay for goods. They send a collect request or a QR code and say "scan to receive money." The victim enters their UPI PIN — and money leaves, not arrives. Other variant: they send a ₹1 test credit, earn your trust, then send a large collect request. Also done through fake "cashback" links from spoofed Paytm/PhonePe SMS.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do Immediately
  • Call 1930 within 60 minutes — provide transaction ID, amount, time
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in under "Financial Fraud"
  • Contact your bank's 24×7 helpline — request transaction reversal
  • File FIR at local Cyber Police Station with transaction screenshots
  • Email pgportal.gov.in if bank refuses to cooperate
🛡️ Prevention
How to Never Fall For This
  • You NEVER need to enter PIN to receive money on UPI
  • Never scan a QR code someone else sends you to "pay you"
  • Reject all collect requests from unknown sources
  • Verify buyer identity via video call before sharing UPI ID

Applicable Law

02
Impersonation Fraud · Highest Average Loss
Digital Arrest Scam
Also known as: "CBI Call Fraud", "Customs Scam", "FedEx Parcel Drug Scam", "ED Arrest Fraud"
🔴 Very High Risk — Avg Loss: ₹5–50 Lakh
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
You receive a call from someone claiming to be from TRAI, FedEx, Customs, CBI, ED, or Narcotics Bureau. They say your Aadhaar/mobile number is linked to a parcel containing drugs/fake passports, or a bank account used for money laundering. You are put on a video call with people in fake police/CBI uniforms in a staged "office." They say you are under "digital arrest" — you must stay on the call and cannot tell anyone. They demand money to "settle" the case. PM Modi specifically warned about this scam in November 2024 on Mann Ki Baat.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • Disconnect immediately — no real agency arrests over video call
  • Call 1930 and report the scammer's number
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in — Report Cyber Crime → Other Cyber Crime
  • If money was paid: call bank immediately + file FIR
  • Report the number to TRAI's DND/Sanchar Saathi at sancharsaathi.gov.in
🛡️ Prevention
The One Rule That Saves You
  • "Digital Arrest" does not exist in Indian law. Full stop.
  • No CBI/ED/Police arrests over WhatsApp or video call
  • Always call 100 or visit your nearest police station to verify
  • Tell your family — especially elderly relatives — about this scam
Step-by-Step Recovery Procedure
1
Immediately
Disconnect the call — hang up now
2
If money paid
Call 1930 with amount and transaction details
3
Within 24 hrs
File on cybercrime.gov.in
4
Within 72 hrs
File FIR at cyber police station
5
For awareness
Report caller ID at sancharsaathi.gov.in
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66D (Cheating by Impersonation using computer resource) · BNS 2023: s.318 (Cheating) · s.319 (Cheating by personating a public servant) · s.140 (Personating public servant) · IPC s.170 (Personating a public servant)
03
Social Engineering · Voice Phishing
OTP Fraud / Vishing
Also known as: "Bank KYC Call", "SBI/HDFC Update Call", "Telecom Disconnection Call"
🔴 High Risk
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
You receive a call from someone impersonating your bank, UIDAI, TRAI, or telecom company. They say: "Your KYC is not updated, your account will be blocked in 24 hours." They ask for your OTP, card number, CVV, or net banking password "to verify your identity." Sometimes they send a fake bank website link. The moment you share the OTP, they drain your account through UPI or net banking transfer.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • Call 1930 immediately with amount and time
  • Call your bank — block your card and internet banking
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in within 24 hours
  • File FIR — mention "impersonation of bank official" specifically
  • Request chargeback from your bank in writing
🛡️ Prevention
The Iron Rule
  • No bank ever calls asking for OTP, PIN, or password
  • Banks do KYC in branch or through their official app — never over phone
  • Hang up. Call your bank's number from the back of your card
  • Enable SMS alert for every transaction
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66C (Identity Theft) · s.66D (Cheating by Impersonation) · BNS 2023: s.318 · RBI Directions on Customer Protection — Limiting Liability of Customers in Unauthorised Electronic Banking Transactions (2017)
04
Investment Fraud · Organised Syndicate
Fake Investment / Trading Fraud
Also known as: "Pig Butchering Scam", "Stock Market Tip Group", "Forex Trading Scam", "Crypto Investment Fraud"
🔴 Very High Risk — Avg Loss: ₹10 Lakh–₹1 Crore
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
A stranger (usually an attractive person on WhatsApp or Instagram) becomes your "friend" over weeks. They mention they made huge profits in stock/crypto trading through a "special platform" or "insider tips group." They add you to a WhatsApp/Telegram group with fake "investors" posting profits. You invest small amounts, see fake profits on the app, invest more. When you try to withdraw, you're asked to pay "tax," "RBI clearance fee," or "processing charges." The app is fake. All profits are fabricated. Your money is gone.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • Stop investing immediately — do not pay any "withdrawal fee"
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in — under "Online Financial Fraud"
  • File FIR — mention "cheating through fake investment platform"
  • Report the fake app/website to MeitY: meity.gov.in
  • Report fake stockbroker to SEBI: scores.sebi.gov.in
  • Check sebi.gov.in for SEBI-registered brokers only
🛡️ Prevention
How to Spot It
  • No genuine investment guarantees fixed returns
  • Check if broker is SEBI/NSE/BSE registered before investing
  • If a stranger contacts you about "investment tips" — it's a scam
  • Never pay fees to withdraw your own money
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66 (Computer Fraud) · s.66D · SEBI Act 1992: s.12A (Prohibition of fraudulent practices) · BNS 2023: s.318 (Cheating) · Prevention of Money Laundering Act 2002
05
Predatory Lending · Privacy Violation
Illegal Loan App Fraud
Also known as: "Instant Loan App Scam", "Chinese Loan App", "Micro-loan Harassment"
🔴 High Risk
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
Apps offer instant personal loans with no documentation. When you install and apply, the app takes permission to access all your contacts, photos, and gallery. They disburse ₹3,000–10,000 but show ₹15,000–20,000 as the "loan amount" in their app. Repayment is demanded in 7 days at 300–500% annual interest. If you delay, they send abusive messages to all your contacts, morphing your photo as a "criminal/defaulter" and sending to your family, employer, and friends. Several suicides have been linked to this harassment.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in — "Cyber Blackmailing/Threatening"
  • File RBI complaint — only RBI-registered NBFCs can give loans
  • Report the app to Google Play/Apple App Store for removal
  • File FIR mentioning harassment, extortion, privacy violation
  • Check RBI's list of registered digital lenders at rbi.org.in
🛡️ Prevention
Before Installing Any Loan App
  • Only use apps from RBI-registered NBFCs or banks
  • Never grant contact/gallery access to any loan app
  • No legitimate lender requests photos for a personal loan
  • Check rbi.org.in/scripts/NBFC for registered companies
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66E (Privacy Violation) · s.67 (Obscene material) · BNS 2023: s.351 (Criminal Intimidation) · s.74 (Assault/Criminal Force) · RBI Digital Lending Guidelines 2022 · DPDP Act 2023 (unauthorized data access/sharing)
06
Impersonation · Aadhaar Misuse
KYC Update / Aadhaar Update Fraud
Also known as: "UIDAI Call Fraud", "eKYC Verification Scam", "Aadhaar Linking Fraud"
🔴 High Risk
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
You receive a call or SMS saying your Aadhaar needs to be updated, or your bank account will be suspended. They ask you to install a screen-sharing app (AnyDesk, TeamViewer, QuickSupport), or share a "KYC verification link." Once you share your screen or click the link, they harvest your Aadhaar OTP, bank credentials, and perform eKYC-based financial transactions, take loans, or open accounts in your name.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • Call 1930 if financial loss occurred
  • Call UIDAI helpline: 1947 to lock your Aadhaar biometrics
  • Lock Aadhaar at myaadhaar.uidai.gov.in → Lock/Unlock Biometrics
  • File FIR mentioning "Aadhaar misuse and identity theft"
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in
🛡️ Prevention
Aadhaar Safety Rules
  • UIDAI never calls asking for Aadhaar OTP
  • Never install screen-sharing apps for "KYC"
  • Lock your Aadhaar biometrics permanently — unlock only when needed at a UIDAI centre
  • Use Virtual ID (VID) instead of actual Aadhaar number
Applicable Law
Aadhaar Act 2016: s.29 (No sharing of identity info) · s.40 (Penalty for impersonation) · IT Act 2000: s.66C (Identity Theft) · s.43 (Unauthorized computer access) · BNS 2023: s.319
07
Search Engine Fraud · Impersonation
Fake Customer Care Number Fraud
Also known as: "Google Top Result Scam", "Fake Helpline Fraud"
🟡 Medium-High Risk
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
When you have a problem with Amazon, Flipkart, your bank, Zomato, or any service — you Google the customer care number. Scammers use SEO tricks and Google Ad campaigns to get their fake number to appear at the top of search results. When you call, they impersonate the real customer care, ask for your OTP, card number, or ask you to install a screen-sharing app to "fix" the problem. They drain your account during the "troubleshooting" session.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • Call 1930 immediately for financial loss
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in
  • Report the fake number to Google: google.com/business → flag as fraud
  • File FIR at cyber police station
🛡️ Prevention
Where to Find Real Numbers
  • Only use numbers from the official app, the back of your debit card, or official website
  • Never Google customer care numbers — scammers dominate search results
  • No genuine customer care installs apps on your phone to "help" you
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66D (Cheating by Impersonation using computer resource) · BNS 2023: s.318 · Consumer Protection Act 2019: s.2(9) (Unfair Trade Practice)
08
Employment Fraud · Advance Fee
Part-Time Job / Task Completion Fraud
Also known as: "Work From Home Scam", "YouTube Like Task Fraud", "Telegram Job Scam"
🔴 High Risk — Most common among students and job-seekers
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
You receive a WhatsApp/Telegram message: "Part-time job — earn ₹500–₹5,000 per day. Just like YouTube videos and subscribe to channels." The first few tasks pay small amounts — building trust. Then you are invited to a "premium task group" where tasks require you to pay ₹1,000–5,000 as deposit to "unlock higher-value tasks." Each payment leads to another deposit demand. The jobs are fake, the task platform is fake, and all deposits are stolen.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in — "Job/Employment Fraud"
  • File FIR at cyber police station with all screenshots
  • Report the Telegram/WhatsApp number to the platform
  • Call 1930 if payment was recent
🛡️ Prevention
Iron Rule for Jobs
  • Legitimate employers never ask you to pay to get a job
  • No real company recruits via unsolicited WhatsApp messages
  • Liking YouTube videos is never a paid job
  • The moment they ask you to pay — it is fraud. Walk away.
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66D · BNS 2023: s.318 (Cheating) · Employment Exchanges (Compulsory Notification of Vacancies) Act 1959 (for formal sector) · Consumer Protection Act 2019
09
Utility Fraud · SMS Phishing
Electricity Bill Disconnection Scam
Also known as: "BESCOM Fraud", "MSEB Scam", "Power Cut Fraud"
🟡 Medium Risk
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
You receive an SMS or WhatsApp saying: "Your electricity connection will be disconnected tonight at 9:30 PM due to non-payment. Call this number immediately." When you call, they impersonate your electricity department, ask you to install a screen-sharing app to "process payment," and drain your account during the session. Or they send a fake payment link that harvests your card details.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • Call 1930 if you paid money
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in
  • File FIR at local police / cyber cell
  • Verify your actual electricity dues on your discom's official app
🛡️ Prevention
Quick Check
  • Electricity departments do not text WhatsApp numbers for payment
  • Check your actual bill status on the official DISCOM app or website
  • Official bill payment links never ask for AnyDesk/TeamViewer installation
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66D · BNS 2023: s.319 (Personating public servant) · Electricity Act 2003: s.135 (Theft of electricity — not applicable here, but relevant to report impersonation of DISCOM officials)
10
Advance Fee Fraud · Classic Scam
Lottery, Prize & Lucky Draw Fraud
Also known as: "KBC Lottery Scam", "Amazon Lucky Draw", "Foreign Lottery Scam", "Nigerian Prince"
🟡 Medium Risk
🎭 How It's Executed
The Modus Operandi
You receive a message: "Congratulations! Your number has won ₹25 Lakh in the KBC/Amazon/Facebook lottery." To claim, you must pay "processing fees," "government tax," "RBI clearance charges," or "legal fees" — each payment is followed by another demand. The prize never arrives. Sometimes a fake "RBI clearance certificate" or "lottery department letter" is produced. These are completely fabricated. No legitimate lottery contacts winners via WhatsApp.
⚖️ Your Remedy
What to Do
  • File on cybercrime.gov.in — "Online Financial Fraud"
  • Call 1930 if recent payment
  • File FIR at cyber police station
  • Report fake KBC prize to Kaun Banega Crorepati's official channels
🛡️ Prevention
The Simplest Rule
  • You cannot win a lottery you never entered
  • Legitimate lotteries never deduct fees from the prize — they deduct TDS from the payment
  • KBC only selects contestants through an on-air phone call — not WhatsApp
  • If it sounds too good to be true — it absolutely is
Applicable Law
IT Act 2000: s.66D · Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act 1978 · BNS 2023: s.318 (Cheating) · Lotteries (Regulation) Act 1998
