01 · Platform Classification

Intermediary Status under the IT Act 2000

Pursuant to Section 2(1)(w), Information Technology Act, 2000 (Act 21 of 2000)
IT Act 2000 · Section 2(1)(w) · Statutory Classification
DPDPIndia.in is an Intermediary under Indian Law
DPDPIndia.in (the "Platform"), operated as an independent research initiative, is classified as an Intermediary under Section 2(1)(w) of the Information Technology Act, 2000. This Platform receives, stores, transmits, and provides services with respect to electronic records constituting policy and legal research content. By virtue of this classification, the Platform is subject to — and complies with — the due diligence requirements prescribed under the IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021, notified on 25 February 2021 by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY).

As an Intermediary, DPDPIndia.in operates within the framework of Section 79 of the IT Act 2000, which grants qualified immunity ("safe harbour") from liability for third-party content, conditional upon compliance with prescribed due diligence obligations. The Platform does not initiate transmission of third-party content; its own editorial research is separately governed by the Research Disclaimer in Section 03 of this page.

DPDPIndia.in may also be classified as a Publisher of News and Current Affairs under Part III of the IT Rules 2021 to the extent it provides analysis and commentary on DPDP policy developments. The Platform monitors its obligations under Part III and will register with the appropriate authority if required.

02 · Section 79 IT Act 2000

Safe Harbour Notice

Under Section 79, Information Technology Act 2000, read with IT Rules 2021, Rule 3
⚠ Important Legal Notice — Please Read
DPDPIndia.in publishes research and educational content on Indian data protection and technology law. This content does not constitute legal advice. Every user of this Platform must independently verify all information and seek advice from qualified legal counsel before acting on anything contained on this Platform. DPDPIndia.in and its researchers, editors, and contributors accept no liability for any errors, omissions, inaccuracies, or outdated information in the research published here.

Scope of Safe Harbour

Section 79(1) of the IT Act 2000 provides that an intermediary shall not be held liable for any third-party information, data, or communication link made available or hosted by it, where the intermediary: (a) does not initiate the transmission, (b) does not select the receiver of the transmission, and (c) does not select or modify the information contained in the transmission. DPDPIndia.in satisfies these conditions with respect to any third-party content or user contributions hosted on the Platform.

Conditions for Safe Harbour — Our Compliance

Safe harbour under Section 79 is conditional on the intermediary observing due diligence as required under the IT Rules 2021. DPDPIndia.in fulfils this requirement by: (i) publishing this Terms of Use and Privacy Policy; (ii) publishing prohibited content rules in the Terms of Use; (iii) appointing a Grievance Officer and publishing contact details on this page; and (iv) maintaining and acting on a 36-hour takedown protocol upon receipt of court orders or government notifications.

What Safe Harbour Does Not Cover

The safe harbour under Section 79 does not protect DPDPIndia.in from liability for its own editorial research content. To limit this exposure, DPDPIndia.in relies on the separate Research Disclaimer (Section 03), which users must accept before accessing the Platform and which clearly negates any duty of care for research errors or omissions.

Additionally, safe harbour is forfeited if the intermediary has actual knowledge of unlawful content (via a court order or government notification) and fails to act on it within 36 hours.

Acceptance of Safe Harbour Terms

By accessing and continuing to use DPDPIndia.in, you confirm that you have read and accepted this Safe Harbour Notice and the Research Disclaimer below. If you do not accept these terms, please discontinue use of this Platform immediately. First-time visitors are presented with a Safe Harbour Notice modal that requires explicit acceptance before accessing any content.

07 · IT Rules 2021, Rule 3(1)(d)

Takedown Protocol

Mandatory under IT Rules 2021, Rule 3(1)(d) — 36-hour compliance window

DPDPIndia.in maintains the following protocol for removing or disabling access to content upon receiving a court order or government notification. The 36-hour window runs from the moment of receipt of the order or notification at the Grievance Officer's contact address.

  1. 01
    Receipt of Order or Notification
    A valid court order or government notification is received by the Grievance Officer via the contact form. The timestamp of receipt is recorded. Private complaints from individuals do not trigger the 36-hour takedown obligation (per Shreya Singhal v. Union of India (2015) 5 SCC 1). Private complaints are addressed separately within 15 days.
  2. 02
    Verification (within 2 hours)
    The order or notification is verified for authenticity — court seal, docket number, or official government letterhead. If the order appears fraudulent or lacks proper identification, the Grievance Officer will seek clarification from the issuing authority.
  3. 03
    Content Identification (within 6 hours)
    The specific content, URL, or communication identified in the order is located on the Platform. If the order is unclear or requires interpretation, legal advice will be sought within this window.
  4. 04
    Removal or Disabling (within 36 hours of receipt)
    The identified content is removed or access to it is disabled within 36 hours of receiving the order. A confirmation is sent to the issuing authority. Records of the order and the action taken are preserved for 180 days.
  5. 05
    Record Retention (180 days)
    All records relating to the takedown — including the original order, the content removed, the action log, and any correspondence — are preserved for 180 days and made available to law enforcement upon receipt of a valid legal demand.
Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) — Priority Protocol
Any content that is prima facie child sexual abuse material or that sexually exploits minors will be removed within 24 hours of identification (whether through a complaint, court order, or proactive detection), and the matter will be reported to law enforcement. No formal court order is required for CSAM removal.
